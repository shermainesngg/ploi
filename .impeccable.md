## Design Context

### Users
Gen-Z fans (18-25) discovering services through creators they follow on social media. Browsing on mobile, impulse-booking energy. They appreciate taste and curation — finding a great salon through a creator they trust feels like insider access.

### Brand Personality
**Curated, intimate, editorial.** Like a well-designed lifestyle magazine that happens to let you book. Quiet confidence, elevated taste. The creator is a tastemaker, not a hype person.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Editorial curation — Kinfolk meets Mindbody
- **Theme**: Light mode — warm cream backgrounds, editorial contrast
- **Typography**: Serif display headings (Brygada 1918) + clean sans body (Schibsted Grotesk)
- **Color**: Warm sienna accent (#c05636) replacing generic rose, warm sand neutrals, sage secondary
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS landing pages, bright pink beauty apps, glassmorphic dashboards
- **References**: Mindbody/ClassPass booking UX, Kinfolk/Cereal magazine editorial aesthetic

### Design Principles
1. **Typography carries the weight** — Let serif headings and careful type hierarchy do the work. Less visual decoration, more typographic precision.
2. **Warmth through restraint** — The palette is warm (sienna, cream, sage) but used sparingly. Accents work because they're rare.
3. **Editorial, not startup** — Left-aligned headings where possible, asymmetric spacing, magazine-like content presentation. No gradient text, no glassmorphism.
4. **Every element earns its space** — No decorative icons above headings, no card-within-card nesting, no identical repeated card grids. Whitespace is a feature.
5. **Mobile-native intimacy** — This is a phone-first experience. Generous touch targets, reading-friendly type, and layouts that feel natural in one hand.
