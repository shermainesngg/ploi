## Design Context

### Users
Gen-Z fans (18-25) discovering services through creators they follow on TikTok/Instagram. Browsing on mobile in Bangkok, impulse-booking energy. They need to trust the page instantly — "Is this legit? Should I enter my card?" The creator recommendation is their trust anchor. High mobile fluency — they expect ClassPass/Klook-level booking UX.

### Brand Personality
**Curated, confident, warm.** Like a premium activity booking platform that happens to be powered by creator recommendations. The serif typography creates editorial warmth that differentiates BRIDGE from generic booking apps, while the layout and interaction patterns feel as polished as Klook or ClassPass. The creator is a tastemaker whose recommendation carries real weight.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Klook meets ClassPass — clean activity booking UX with editorial warmth
- **Theme**: Light mode — warm cream backgrounds (`#faf8f5`), editorial contrast
- **Typography**: Serif display headings (Brygada 1918) for warmth + clean sans body (Schibsted Grotesk) for scannability. Serif differentiates BRIDGE from generic booking apps.
- **Color**: BRIDGE red (`#DC2751`) as primary accent. Warm sand neutrals (`#faf8f5` bg, `#f2ede7` surface). Hero gradient: warm red-to-orange (`#DC2751` → `#F97316`). Accent used sparingly — CTAs, selected states, trust badges, brand moments.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS landing pages, bright pink beauty apps, glassmorphic dashboards, bland white booking pages with blue CTAs
- **References**: Klook (bold trust badges, clean service cards, sticky CTA, "X booked today" urgency), ClassPass (studio discovery, clean card grids, premium feel), Mindbody (booking flow polish)

**What BRIDGE borrows and what it doesn't:**
- BORROW: ClassPass's subtle shadow values, pill-shaped CTA energy, two-font pairing, restrained accent use
- BORROW: Klook's trust badge prominence, left-right card split, sticky CTA, "X booked" urgency
- DON'T BORROW: ClassPass's monochromatic coldness (BRIDGE is warm), Klook's corporate orange (BRIDGE red is more distinctive), either platform's lack of creator social proof (that's BRIDGE's unique layer)

### Design Principles

1. **Trust signals are first-class citizens** — "X booked this week", "Recommended by N creators", creator attribution bar — all must be visible without scrolling. Klook-level bold pill badges, not buried text. Social proof converts.

2. **Serif warmth, booking-app speed** — Brygada 1918 headings give BRIDGE editorial personality that generic booking apps lack. But the interaction patterns (service cards, date picker, time slots, sticky CTA) must feel as fast and frictionless as Klook. Typography carries the brand; layout carries the conversion.

3. **The Klook split** — Service cards use left-right layout: name/description/duration on the left, price + Book CTA stacked on the right. Clean, scannable, one-thumb-friendly. Price is bold and prominent — no hunting for it.

4. **BRIDGE red is the conversion color** — `#DC2751` appears on CTAs, selected states (dates, time slots), the sticky Book Now, and trust signal badges. Used as 10% accent against 60% warm neutrals and 30% text/borders. Rare enough to draw the eye, consistent enough to build brand recognition.

5. **Mobile-native intimacy** — This is a phone-first experience. Generous touch targets (48px minimum), reading-friendly type, layouts that feel natural in one hand. The sticky Book Now is always visible. Booking flow feels like swiping through stories — fast, fluid, no friction.

### Shop Booking Page — Specific Direction

**Hero**: Bold trust badges as pill badges (backdrop-blur on hero image). "Instant Booking" and "X booked this week" should feel like Klook's confidence signals. Category and price badges already exist — amplify them.

**Creator Bar**: The creator attribution card immediately after the hero is the key trust differentiator. It replaces Klook's "Verified Activity" badge — the creator IS the verification. Keep it prominent with the BRIDGE accent tag.

**Creators Callout**: Instagram Stories-style stacked avatars with "N creators recommend this place" — this is social proof at scale. The gradient strip at the bottom gives it energy.

**Service Cards**: Klook-style left-right split. Left: service name (bold), description (muted, 2-line clamp), duration with clock icon. Right: price (large, bold) stacked above compact "Book" button in BRIDGE red. Cards have subtle shadows, rounded-2xl corners, clean borders.

**Featured Service Card**: When arriving via creator link with a featured service — this is the hero moment. Creator's avatar, their recommendation, video thumbnail, and a full-width "Book this treatment" CTA. More emotional and editorial than the regular service cards.

**Booking Modal**: Bottom sheet with wizard steps. Date picker as horizontal scrolling date chips (selected = BRIDGE red fill). Time slots as pill buttons in a flex-wrap grid. Details form is clean and minimal. Confirmed screen has the accent checkmark and booking summary.

**Sticky CTA**: Fixed bottom bar with gradient fade from background. Full-width "Book Now" in BRIDGE red with strong shadow. Always visible, never obstructed.
